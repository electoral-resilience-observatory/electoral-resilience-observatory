About

Evidence-oriented analysis of electoral resilience

The Electoral Resilience Observatory provides structured analysis of how election management bodies and democratic institutions respond to disruption, uncertainty, and emerging electoral risks.

01

Project mission

To build a research and policy platform for understanding electoral threats, institutional responses, and democratic resilience across election cycles.

02

Scope

The Observatory analyzes EMB reports, parliamentary oversight reports, observer reports, post-election reviews, legal reforms, operational disruptions, and public confidence issues.

03

Analytical pillars

Findings are organized around environmental, societal, technological/informational, and institutional/logistical threats and resilience adaptations.

Voting and Resilience

Electoral participation as the foundation of resilience

Electoral resilience begins with public participation, accessible voting, reliable administration, and institutional capacity to maintain confidence under pressure.

Voting · Participation · Trust

Accessible and trusted electoral participation

The Observatory tracks how institutions protect voting access, manage disruption, respond to emerging threats, and sustain public confidence in electoral processes.

Method

Transparent coding and comparative analysis

The Observatory uses a structured coding framework to identify threats, responses, learning, adaptation, and recommendations in election-related reports.

01

Collect reports

Gather EMB reports, parliamentary reviews, election observer reports, and post-election evaluations.

02

Code evidence

Extract evidence of electoral threats, operational disruptions, organizational learning, and institutional responses.

03

Structure findings

Organize coded findings into comparative tables, matrices, indicators, and policy-relevant summaries.

04

Visualize resilience

Produce dashboards, heat maps, scorecards, timelines, and comparative visualizations.
